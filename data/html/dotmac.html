Raising the competitive bar still higher, Apple just announced iLife '05, featuring major new versions of iPhoto, iMovie, iDVD and GarageBand and including the latest version of iTunes.

iPhoto 5 adds advanced editing tools so you can perfect your photos right within iPhoto, and it supports uncompressed RAW photos so high-end users can exploit all the image data their cameras can capture. There's also a new advanced slideshow builder, an entirely new way to create affordable ($3.99 and up) hardcover and softcover photo books, and the price of 4 x 6 prints ordered via iPhoto has been cut in half to just 19 cents each.

With iMovie, Apple revolutionized movie making by empowering anyone with a Mac to import and edit digital video into finished movies. Now iMovie HD leads the revolution again with support for stunning high definition video (HDV) from the new generation of HDV camcorders. iMovie HD also introduces the easiest way ever to make a movie: just a few clicks, and the new Magic iMovie feature automatically imports your video into separate clips and adds titles, transitions and music, all in one step. iDVD 5 adds 15 amazing new themes featuring moving drop zones just like the latest Hollywood DVDs, and the new OneStep DVD feature lets you create DVDs by simply plugging in a DV camcorder and clicking a button.

One year ago GarageBand put a complete recording studio on the Mac. Now GarageBand 2 adds 8-track recording, so you can record your entire band at once, and it displays and lets you edit musical notation in real time. And with Apple's growing family of GarageBand Jam Packs, including the latest, Jam Pack 4: Symphony Orchestra, you can create professional-sounding music in just about any style you can imagine. iLife '05 also includes iTunes 4.7.1, the latest version of the world's best digital music jukebox software with the iTunes Music Store inside. iTunes provides seamless integration with Apple's complete family of popular iPods, including the iPod shuffle.

iLife '05 will be available in the US January 22 and worldwide January 29 through the Apple Store (store.apple.com), at Apple's retail stores and through Apple Authorized Resellers for a suggested retail price of US$79. Find out more about new iLife '05.

 

Apple has also unveiled iWork '05, productivity software designed from the ground up to take advantage of both Mac OS X and iLife to help users create, present and publish amazing documents and presentations with style. iWork '05 introduces Pages, an innovative word processor that makes it easy for anyone to create great-looking newsletters, reports, letters and more. iWork '05 also features Keynote 2, a major new version of Apple's presentation software that makes it even easier to create professional presentations.

Both Pages and Keynote 2 exploit the advanced typography and graphics engine of Mac OS X; provide instant access to iLife photos, movies and music; and include a collection of themes and templates that make it easy to produce stunning results in minutes. iWork '05 imports and exports AppleWorks, Microsoft Word and PowerPoint files and supports a wide range of industry-standard file formats including Adobe PDF, Adobe Photoshop, TIFF, JPG and QuickTime. Keynote 2 now also includes the ability to export presentations to Macromedia Flash.

iWork '05 will be available in the US January 22 and worldwide January 29 through the Apple Store (store.apple.com), at Apple's retail stores and through Apple Authorized Resellers for a suggested retail price of US$79. Find out more about new iWork '05.
